Personas

Personas can help designers better understand potential users of the interface. I created two personas, Patient Pam and Stressed Susan, to represent the potential user base of the Ten One Tea House self-checkout kiosk.

How can I best replicate the historical theme of the original page while increasing the color contrast and overall, provide more visual clarity? I chose a beige/red theme to fit the brick architecture of many of the buildings in the neighborhood.

Compare & Contrast: Although these personas are demographically different (occupation, age, stage in life), they are the same gender, view boba as a special treat, and both associate a feeling of excitement with getting boba. Additionally, both personas feel overwhelmed or stressed, but for different reasons. While Susan is dealing with stress about her college class workload, Pam is concerned about bonding and communicating with her daughter. Another key difference between the two personas is that Susan is more familiar with the self-checkout kiosk in Ten One Tea House and the Ten One Tea House teas and boba than Pam.

Storyboarding

To visualize how one of my personas, Stressed Susan, might interact with this interface, I created a storyboard to illustrate Susan leaving her dorm to get bubble tea, using the self-checkout kiosk, and receiving her drink.
